Toolkit

Inspired by existing research papers that have applied natural language processing (NLP) to assist human fact-checking, we present following ideas. Each idea is displayed with a text-based storyboard and a real-time prototype. Please read the storyboard description to understand how NLP is adopted to assist fact-checking and explore prototypes to make sense of its implementations.

1. Detect checkable claims

Description: this idea aims to use an existing annotation category to automatedly classify and detect checkable new claims. The category is developed by experts at Full Fact, based on their needs of checking claims by topics and routing different types of claims to the right dataset for the downstream fact-checking tasks.

Objective: this idea aims to lead the discussion of how to objectively determine "checkable" claims based on different claim-selection criteria across fact-checking organizations.

2. Detect check-worthy claims on social media

Description: this idea aims to use different social media metrics to detect the virality and susceptibility of tweets based on crowd's activities, such as people's comments and retweets. By analyzing different linguistic features from tweets' comments, including sentiment, political ideology, and stances, the system helps to detect if the claim is worthy of fact-checking. The check-worthy result combine effects from both social media metrics and linguistic features.

Objective: this idea aims to lead the discussion of how to define virality and susceptibility of social media posts, how to objectively quantify those social media metrics, and how to combine linguistic features to find check-worthy claims.

3. Generate news article briefs for fast-reading

Description: this idea aims to use an extractive summarization method to highlight sentences from the article. These sentences are compressed into a list of summary brief and considered to be part of a summary.

Objective: this idea aims to lead the discussion of how summarization helps to assist fact-checking across different separated tasks, such as fast-reading in claim investigation.

4. Robocheck simple claims by searching evidence

Description: this idea aims to retrieve relevant articles according to a claim and automatedly verify the claim based on stances of these retrieval articles.

Objective: this idea aims to lead the discussion of what kinds of claims are appropriate for machine to check and how fact-checkers review these robocheck results efficiently.
